## 1. Main Question

Is SEA (Sea Limited) building a durable, owned AI/agentic-commerce advantage for
Shopee, or is its agentic-commerce direction dependent on external AI platforms
(OpenAI, Google) that could commoditize Shopee into a supplier behind someone
else's shopping agent?

## 2. Scope

In scope:

- Sea Limited AI initiatives across Shopee (e-commerce), Monee (fintech), and Garena (games).
- Agentic commerce: AI agents that discover products and/or complete purchases (Shopee-in-ChatGPT, Google agentic shopping prototype).
- Seller-side AI tooling (ChatGPT for Business for Shopee sellers) and ShopeeVIP AI bundling.
- SEA in-house AI capability building (AI Centre of Excellence, Codex hackathons).
- Southeast Asia + Brazil markets where Shopee operates.

Out of scope:

- Non-SEA Southeast Asian AI efforts except as competitive comparison (e.g. Lazada, GoTo, Grab).
- Pure infrastructure/model-provider economics unless they change Shopee's position.

## 4. Research Phases

- Phase 1 — Map: catalogue SEA's announced AI initiatives by segment (Shopee, Monee, Garena) and by layer (discovery agent, seller tooling, infrastructure).
- Phase 2 — Anchor cases: Shopee-in-ChatGPT (OpenAI) and the Google–Sea agentic shopping prototype.
- Phase 3 — Taxonomy: classify each initiative as owned vs partner-dependent, consumer vs seller, discovery vs transaction.
- Phase 4 — Analysis: assess whether the agent layer (and its take-rate/ad economics) is owned by Shopee or by the AI platform.
