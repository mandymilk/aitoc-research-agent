Executive Summary

Sea Limited is moving Shopee toward agentic commerce primarily **through

partnerships with large AI platforms** rather than a self-owned shopping agent.

As of 22 June 2026, the Shopee app is live inside ChatGPT across eight markets,

and the same Sea–OpenAI partnership pushes ChatGPT for Business to Shopee sellers.

In parallel, a February 2026 Google–Sea agreement reportedly explores an AI

agentic shopping prototype on Shopee, while Sea has stood up an in-house AI Centre

of Excellence in Singapore. The open strategic question: who owns the agent and

its discovery/ad economics — Shopee, or the AI platform in front of it?

What Changed

Discovery is shifting off Shopee's own surfaces and into third-party AI

assistants (ChatGPT now; a Google prototype next). Monetization signals point to

seller tooling and membership bundling rather than a consumer AI subscription.

Analysis

  • H1 (partner-dependent agent layer): leans supported. Core agentic surfaces are OpenAI and Google, not a Shopee-owned agent.
  • H2 (hybrid consumer + seller AI): leans supported. Value shows up as ChatGPT for Business + ShopeeVIP, not a paid consumer AI tier.
  • H3 (in-house build): only the AI CoE supports this; core UX still runs on partners.

The decisive variable is control of the agent layer's economics (ads, take rate,

data). If ChatGPT/Google own the high-intent discovery moment, Shopee risks

becoming a fulfilment supplier behind someone else's agent.

Unit Economics Implication

If discovery migrates to third-party agents, Shopee's ad-load and take-rate on

AI-originated orders may compress, and customer-acquisition leverage could move to

the AI platform. Needs data on referral/rev-share terms in the ChatGPT flow.

Open Questions

  • Who captures the ad/take-rate on Shopee orders that originate inside ChatGPT?
  • Does Shopee ship (or plan) an owned in-app shopping agent at comparable reach?
  • Is the Google agentic prototype live, and how does it differ from the OpenAI route?
