# Research Questions And Hypotheses

## Main Question

Can AI-to-C products become durable standalone businesses, or do they need large-company subsidy, bundled distribution, or non-consumer revenue to survive?

## Research Posture

The project should try to disprove its starting hypothesis. Evidence from standalone challengers should be actively sought before concluding that large companies have the only viable model.

## Decision Questions

- What consumer AI categories have high enough retention to justify subscription?
- Which user jobs create willingness to pay above compute and support cost?
- Where does usage-based pricing improve gross margin without killing user growth?
- Which products can lower customer acquisition cost through owned distribution?
- Which companies can use AI-to-C as a strategic layer even when direct profit is weak?
- Does business/consumer convergence make AI-to-C more sustainable?

## Falsification Tests

- If a standalone consumer AI company shows durable retention, paid conversion, and positive gross margin without platform subsidy, H1 weakens.
- If usage tiers reduce heavy-user losses without increasing churn, H4 strengthens.
- If bundled AI products drive measurable upgrades to existing paid plans, H5 strengthens.
- If ads become a meaningful revenue line without damaging trust or product quality, hybrid consumer AI becomes more viable.
- If model inference costs fall faster than user demand expands, direct subscriptions become more plausible.

## Required Challenger Tests

The research sample should include standalone or non-platform challengers such as:

- Perplexity
- Character.AI
- Replika
- AI-native education tools
- AI-native creator tools
- Regional consumer AI products outside the US big-tech stack

These are required because a sample dominated by Microsoft, Google, ByteDance, Meta, or Apple would bias the research toward the large-company subsidy thesis.
