# Survival Classification

## `standalone_viable`

The product appears capable of surviving as an independent consumer business.

Evidence usually includes:

- Strong paid conversion or ARPU.
- Durable retention.
- Controlled inference cost.
- Reasonable acquisition cost.
- Clear willingness to pay.

## `hybrid_viable`

The product appears viable, but only because direct consumer revenue is combined with another revenue source.

Examples:

- Subscription plus ads.
- Subscription plus enterprise.
- Consumer usage plus API revenue.
- Marketplace plus paid tiers.

## `strategic_subsidy`

The product may not need direct standalone profitability because it supports a larger business.

Examples:

- Microsoft using Copilot to defend Microsoft 365.
- Google using Gemini to defend search, Android, and Workspace.
- ByteDance using Doubao to support consumer ecosystem engagement.

## `not_yet_viable`

The product has user demand, but current evidence does not support durable economics.

Common signs:

- Weak retention.
- High inference cost.
- Unclear willingness to pay.
- Heavy dependence on novelty.
- Paid plan does not map to repeated high-value jobs.

## `unknown`

There is not enough reliable evidence.

Use this classification when the product is important but evidence is mostly rumor, anecdote, or incomplete.
