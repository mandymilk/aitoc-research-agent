# Business Model Taxonomy

## Direct Consumer Revenue

- Freemium subscription: free base product with paid plan.
- Tiered usage subscription: paid tiers segmented by limits, model quality, speed, or capability.
- Credit pack: pay for compute, generations, or tasks.
- One-time purchase: rare for AI services because ongoing inference cost remains.

## Indirect Consumer Revenue

- Ads-supported free tier: free usage monetized through advertising.
- Marketplace: creators, bots, workflows, or agents generate revenue share.
- Affiliate/commerce: AI drives purchase, booking, or transaction revenue.
- Data/licensing: consumer usage supports licensing or answer/data partnerships.

## Bundled Revenue

- Productivity bundle: AI attached to an existing subscription such as office software.
- OS/device bundle: AI improves device or operating-system stickiness.
- Search/browser bundle: AI defends or grows search and browser usage.
- Telecom/hardware bundle: AI included with device, cloud, or carrier plans.

## Cross-Subsidy Revenue

- Enterprise pull-through: consumer usage builds brand and demand for business plans.
- API/developer revenue: consumer product improves model adoption and developer demand.
- Cloud/platform subsidy: company can absorb AI cost to defend cloud or ecosystem value.
- Strategic defense: AI reduces churn or protects an existing profit pool.

## Survival Implication

Direct consumer revenue is cleanest but hardest. Bundled and cross-subsidy models are more realistic for large platforms. Hybrid models may become the default because they distribute cost and revenue across more than one surface.
