# Research Idea: Are usage credits becoming the default AI-to-C margin-control mechanism?

Date created: 2026-07-13
Status: open
Priority: high

## Question

Are per-user "usage credits" (metered allowances that gate heavy usage and advanced models/agents) becoming the default margin-control mechanism across AI-to-C products, rather than flat-rate unlimited subscriptions?

## Why Now

Microsoft's April 2026 Copilot support page confirms an "AI Credits" system across individual Microsoft 365 plans plus pay-as-you-go agents for business (evidence-2026-07-13-microsoft-per-microsoft-s-copilot-support-page-last-updat). If a hyperscaler that can absorb inference cost still meters consumer usage, credit metering may be structural to the category.

## Target Business Model

- Metered subscription / usage-credit hybrid
- Bundle-attached AI (productivity subscription + AI credits)

## Candidate Products

- Microsoft Copilot (confirmed credit metering)
- ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini consumer tiers (to verify)
- Regional players (Doubao) (to verify)

## Evidence Needed

- Exact AI Credit allowances by Microsoft 365 plan (official "AI credits and limits" page).
- Whether standalone challengers meter with credits vs. message caps vs. unlimited.
- Any published gross-margin rationale linking credits to heavy-user cost.

## Possible Thesis Impact

Strengthens H4 and the working hypothesis that AI-to-C viability depends on capping/shaping usage to protect gross margin. Would not by itself resolve standalone viability (H1).
